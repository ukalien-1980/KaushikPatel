How I Lead

Leadership Philosophy

01

Prove before you ask.

Build the prototype. Run the pilot. Have the data before the meeting. Nobody can say no to results that already exist. That's how a $30 Arduino became a $120M platform in 13 months. The network expansion was approved before the political window could close.

02

Trust is the product.

Every system I've built, from AI platforms to IoT sensors to LLM capabilities, only delivers value when humans trust it enough to act on it. Technical excellence is table stakes. Earning trust from workers, sales reps, regulators, and executives is the actual job.

03

Find the signal others miss.

The Works With Alexa certification program came from a pattern buried in support tickets that nobody had connected. The best product opportunities don't announce themselves, they hide in data anomalies, customer complaints, and the gaps between what teams measure and what actually matters.

The Through-Line

Before anyone called it agentic.

The term is new. The work isn't.

In 2018, I built a conversational system for 350,000 Amazon warehouse associates to report safety issues in natural language, hands-free and mid-shift, across dozens of languages. It asked follow-up questions, routed the issue, and took action. Nobody called it agentic then. We called it a safety reporting tool.

At JPMorgan Chase, I built a production system with a conversational interface, multi-step autonomous workflows, and live data retrieval, inside one of the most regulated environments in financial services. The specifics are confidential. The pattern is the same.

The problems that make agentic AI hard in practice, earning trust from skeptical users, operating inside real constraints, measuring adoption not just deployment, are ones I've been working through for years.
